<p class="club-lead-intro"><strong>Coupe du Québec</strong> is the main men&rsquo;s knockout cup in Québec. All 18 <a href="/competitions/la-ligue/">La Ligue</a> clubs take part alongside the 12 teams from Ligue1 Québec. The cup is separate from the league season and typically begins in early summer, with rounds continuing into the autumn.</p>
</div>


## History
The cup was introduced in parallel with the launch of professional football at the top of the Québec pyramid. It gives clubs from different levels a single trophy to chase and opens the door for runs by semi-professional sides against <a href="/competitions/la-ligue/">La Ligue</a> opposition. The first final, in <a href="/competitions/coupe_du_québec/2023/">2023</a>, was won by <a href="/clubs/petite-bourgogne/">Petite-Bourgogne</a> after a penalty shootout.

#### Gateway to the North-American cup
With the creation of La Ligue and the Coupe du Québec, the CONCACAF granted one qualification spot to the winner of the Coupe du Québec. Initial demands from the Quebec organisation were that the winner of La Ligue would automatically gain a spot, but CONCACAF ruled that qualification through the provincial cup and the national cup was more logical to be able to include more clubs.

#### Venue
The cup final is played at the Olympic Stadium in Montréal as a neutral venue. 

## Competition format
The tournament is straight knockout. Ties are single-leg fixtures, with the draw determining home advantage from round to round through to the final. If scores are level after ninety minutes, the winner is decided by penalty shootouts immediately.

#### Schedule
The two finalists from the previous edition automatically qualify to the Second Round. The months in which rounds are played are traditional, with exact dates subject to each year's calendar.
